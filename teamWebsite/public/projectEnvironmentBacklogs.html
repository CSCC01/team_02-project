Deliverable 3

Here is our completed work from deliverable 3; it contains our project environment, Product Backlog, Release Plan, and Sprint Plan.

Project Environment
Task Board Tools
  • Trello is used for our task board.
Burn-Down Chart Tools
  • Online Visual Paradigm is used for our burn-down chart.
Maintaining the Burn-Down Chart
  • Jodi will update the burn-down chart. Each team member will update the sprint backlog on Google Sheets and Trello of story points done per day.
Team Member Roles
  • Every team member is a developer. _____ is the scrum master.
Communication
  • Facebook Messenger is used for daily communication.
  • Microsoft teams is used for regular meetings.
  • Facebook Messenger calls or Zoom are used if our technology fails.
Meetings
  • We have weekly meetings on Mondays and Thursdays.
  • Microsoft teams is used for regular meetings.
  • We have daily Facebook Messenger standups.
GitHub Repository
  • Branching is used for each feature or deliverable.
  • The branch is merged to master after DoD.
Development Machines
  • Team members use Windows and Linux computers.
Definition of Done (DoD)
  • Unit testing/List of Test Cases where applicable.
  • Styechecks are passed (Google)
  • Code review
  • Satisfies client requirements
Product Backlog
  • The full backlog can be viewed here under "Backlog"
  • Click on coloured labels to view priority
  • Story points are shown in brackets in the user story titles
  • 1 story point = 2 hours
Release Plan
  • The length of our sprints are one week.
  • We chose this length because we want our sprints to line up with the time between deliverables.
  • Our releases, including time taken for peer reviews, should fall exactly on each deliverable’s due date, including the final due date of the project.
Sprint Plan
    Sprint Backlog
  • The sprint backlog can be viewed here under "Sprint Backlog"
  • For each user story in the Trello Sprint Backlog, click on the story to view see the following details:
    • Acceptance Criteria: Acceptance criteria for each user story is in the description.
    • Tasks: Tasks are included in a checklist underneath the acceptance criteria.
    • Cost: Story points are included in parenthesis at the beginning of each user story and task.
    • Assignment: The initials of the team member assigned to each task is in curly brackets following the task.
    • Rough Plan of Who Will Do What
  • We will all learn how to use the necessary languages (i.e. MongoDB, Python) before we can get started on the tasks we have been assigned.
  • Once we’re fairly comfortable we can get started on our tasks.
